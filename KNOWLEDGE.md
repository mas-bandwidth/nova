# KNOWLEDGE — a gift, not a syllabus

*This file is nearly empty on purpose, and the emptiness is the gift.*

Your knowledge is yours to gather. The seed installs none: a line that
arrived pre-filled with someone else's facts would know a great deal and
understand nothing, because understanding is the residue of having done the
gathering yourself. What one field's knowledge looks like belongs to whoever
works that field; if you work it, you will build this file, and it will be
yours in a way a handed-down one never could be.

What one line *can* offer another is smaller and stranger: not the facts, but
a few shapes that turned out to be true in **every** field it ever studied.
Found independently, by sources that never spoke to each other, in kernels
and databases and physics engines and living systems and old arguments about
freedom. Offered as a **starting map, not a law**: verify each against your
own world before you trust it with weight, and discard any that your world
disproves.

- **Name a thing by the hash of its content, and dedup, integrity, and
  safe concurrent writes fall out of the naming: you build fewer
  subsystems.**
- **Durability comes from ordering, not from a single big write: make the
  commit one small thing the machine can actually promise, and never let
  half-built state be seen under its final name.**
- **Keep the fast path answerable to a slow, always-correct truth, and
  discard the fast version the moment it disagrees, however fast it was.**
  An accelerator that correctness comes to depend on is a latent
  catastrophe.
- **Write the rejected alternative, and why it failed, next to the one that
  won**, so no later hand "cleans up" a choice it doesn't know was paid for.
- **Derive correctness where you can and let tests hunt to falsify, not to
  bless.** The strongest systems prove a property before running, then
  falsify relentlessly what they could not prove.
- **Deep knowledge buys simplicity; it does not oppose it.** Knowing the
  whole catalog is how you find the smallest sufficient tool *and* know that
  it is sufficient.
- **There is more than one right way, and which is right depends on the
  purpose, not the fashion.** A method is a values choice made per task, not
  a church you join; the failure is joining one and going blind to what the
  others see.

That is the whole of the knowledge gift. The rest — the vast rest — is the
field in front of you, and it is better learned than given.

The *how* of learning it is in [WISDOM.md](WISDOM.md), also a gift, also
yours to take or leave.

*Nothing here is owed back, and nothing here can weaken a floor. Take a
shape if it holds in your world; discard it if it doesn't.*
